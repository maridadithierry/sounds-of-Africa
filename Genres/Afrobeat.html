Afrobeat

Description

Afrobeat is a genre of music that originated in West Africa, particularly in Nigeria, during the 1960s and 1970s. It blends traditional African rhythms with jazz, funk, and highlife. Complex percussion patterns and political lyrics are hallmarks of Afrobeat.

History and Key Characteristics

The genre was popularized by Fela Kuti, who used music as a tool for activism. Key instruments include drums, saxophones, and electric guitars. Afrobeat is known for its long, danceable grooves and socially conscious messages.

Prominent Artists

  • Wizkid
  • Burna Boy
  • Fela Kuti
